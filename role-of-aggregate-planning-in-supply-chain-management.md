## Key Strategies For Effective Aggregate Planning
Here are some effective strategies and methods that will help you predict the upcoming demands and plan the resources and procurement accordingly.

### Aggregate Planning Strategies
* **Chase Strategy:** It aims to adjust production rates to match the market demand. The **process of aggregate planning** includes lowering the inventory level as per the customer demand.
* **Level Strategy:** This includes maintaining consistent production while observing the market demand. You need to increase or decrease the production as per the consumer requirement and maintain a consistent workflow.
* **Hybrid Strategy:** This **aggregate planning techniques** is the combination of chase and level strategies for optimal results. You need to maintain the required inventory level while consistently producing to match consumer needs.

### Methods For Aggregate Planning
Here are the most common and easy **methods for aggregate planning** that will help you balance supply and demand with minimized costs and improve the overall manufacturing operations.
* **Graphical And Charting Methods:** It includes a graphical representation to compare the needs through a trial and error approach. The chart and graphics also include the cut-and-try method, which includes manual practice to understand the needs of the workforce, [production planning and control](/production-planning-and-control), and inventory level.
* **Mathematical Models:** It includes numerous **methods for aggregate planning,** such as linear programming, to identify the number of workers and machines to be used for optimal results. It also includes integer programming, global programming, and transportation models to simplify supply management.
* **Simulation Models:** It is a computer-based technique that helps SME manufacturers demonstrate real-world processes virtually. The technique may include Monte Carlo Simulation, System Dynamics Models, and What-If Analysis.
