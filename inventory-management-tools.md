## Best Inventory Management Tools and Techniques
Inventory control tools support many inventory management techniques. You need to pick which inventory control tools and techniques work the best for your manufacturing business. Let us understand the 5 key Inventory control techniques:

### 1. ABC Analysis
[ABC analysis](/blogs/abc-analysis-in-inventory-management) is based on the value of materials. This analysis covers the “A” category as the highest value items, and "B" and "C" as moderate to low-value items. Now, you have to make sure that the “A" list is maintained at safe levels and so on.

### 2. FIFO, LIFO
[First-In, First-Out (FIFO), Last-In, First-Out (LIFO)](/blogs/fifo-vs-lifo) help to determine the [cost of goods sold](/blogs/cost-of-goods-sold) within inventory management. In FIFO, the first items in the inventory are sold first, whereas the items added most recently are sold first in LIFO.

### 3. Traceability
Data traceability helps you trace the location, age and quality of your items. The traceability feature is a great addition that helps you with customer fulfillment. It is the ability to track a product’s journey from its origin through its production and distribution process to its final destination.

### 4. Cycle Monitoring
[Cycle monitoring](/blogs/inventory-cycle-count) is a way of monitoring inventory continuously. Through this method, the movement of materials is tracked, right from the time they are issued till they reach the warehouse as finished goods. This will alert you of its non-availability or backlog.

### 5. Identification
The tool also lets you use a barcode or RFID to find the product and sync the data in the system. The status of any raw material, semi-finished goods, or finished goods is available instantly to the department managers at one glance. This saves time consumed and reduces errors.

## Inventory Management Best Practices
Here are the most effective inventory management practices in the manufacturing industry:

### 1. Effective monitoring
An inventory management tool keeps track of materials, goods being packed, or finished goods being dispatched. It pinpoints the raw materials lying around too. It helps you manage overcrowding of materials resulting in loss or damage.

### 2. Quicker deliveries
One of the biggest factors in retaining customers is timely deliveries. Unexpected delays in manufacturing, shortages of raw materials, and unplanned interruptions can cause order delays. It helps you hold neither too much stock nor too little without increasing holding costs or lost orders.

### 3. Measure Performance
You can also set standards and methods to measure results. You can analyze gathered data and take steps to improve. Measures can be set for variations in inventory, cost of holding inventory, age of non-moving stocks, vendor performance, cycle time etc.

### 4. Never out of stock
Getting stuck between customer demand and material stockout is a situation you must avoid at all costs. You should make sure to maintain a minimum inventory. A good inventory tracking system will make sure that you do not run out of any interruptions.

### 5. Proactive steps
This software helps you understand the company inventory. It lets you be prepared and avoid extra costs. You can take intelligent steps through proper inventory management tools for a small business to fail-safe your manufacturing business.
