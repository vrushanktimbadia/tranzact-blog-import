# FSN Analysis in Inventory Management

**TL;DR:** FSN analysis helps manufacturers classify inventory into fast, slow, and non-moving items to reduce costs, improve cash flow, and avoid stockouts.

FSN Analysis in inventory management is important for maintaining business performance and profit, especially in manufacturing.

## What is FSN Analysis?

FSN analysis classifies inventory based on movement rate so businesses can control stock better.

## How Does FSN Analysis Work?

Items are grouped by sales or usage over a defined period, enabling better stocking and purchasing decisions.

## Importance of FSN Analysis

It helps reduce holding costs, improve cash flow, and ensure availability of high-demand items.

## How to Conduct FSN Analysis

1. List inventory items  
2. Collect usage data  
3. Calculate annual usage  
4. Classify into F, S, and N categories  

## Advantages and Limitations

FSN analysis improves planning but depends on accurate data and stable demand.

## FAQs

### What is FSN analysis?
A method to classify inventory by movement rate.

### Why is FSN analysis useful?
It prevents excess stock and stockouts.

### What are fast-moving items?
Items with high sales frequency.

### What are non-moving items?
Items that remain unsold for long periods.

### Is FSN analysis suitable for SMEs?
Yes, it is especially useful for manufacturing SMEs.
