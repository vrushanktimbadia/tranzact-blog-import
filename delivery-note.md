# What Is a Delivery Note?

**TL;DR:** A delivery note records what goods are sent, to whom, and when. It helps Indian businesses confirm deliveries, resolve disputes, and streamline invoicing without confusion.

A delivery note is an operational document used during the movement of goods. It clearly lists what has been shipped, along with sender and receiver details, so both parties stay aligned.

## What Is a Delivery Note?

A delivery note is a document that accompanies goods during shipment. It typically includes shipment date, sender and receiver details, and a description and quantity of items.

Its main role is to ensure the correct items reach the correct destination. By documenting shipment contents clearly, businesses reduce delivery errors and operational friction.

## What Is the Purpose of a Delivery Note?

A delivery note primarily acts as a record of shipment contents, but it also supports several business-critical functions:

- **Shipment clarity:** Records shipment date, sender, receiver, and item details.
- **Dispute resolution:** Acts as reference evidence if items are missing or incorrect.
- **Proof of delivery:** Signed acknowledgement confirms goods receipt.
- **Process efficiency:** Helps accounting teams match deliveries with invoices.

In short, delivery notes reduce confusion and improve accountability in logistics.

## Types of Delivery Notes

Different business scenarios require different delivery note formats:

- **Standard Delivery Note:** Basic shipment details including sender, receiver, and items.
- **Packing Slip:** Focuses on item quantities and descriptions inside the package.
- **Commercial Invoice:** Used for international shipments with value and tax details.
- **Return Note:** Documents returned goods with reasons and quantities.
- **Delivery Confirmation Note:** Confirms receipt through signature or acknowledgement.

## What Should a Delivery Note Include?

A complete delivery note generally contains:

- **Date of shipment**
- **Sender details** (name, address, contact)
- **Recipient details** (name, address, contact)
- **Item description** (quantity, weight, SKU if applicable)
- **Delivery instructions**
- **Order or reference number**
- **Signature or receipt acknowledgement**

These elements ensure traceability and legal clarity.

## Functions of a Delivery Note

Delivery notes support day-to-day operations in multiple ways:

- **Record-keeping:** Maintains accurate shipment history.
- **Information sharing:** Keeps both sender and receiver aligned.
- **Dispute handling:** Serves as evidence during shipment issues.
- **Tracking:** Helps monitor shipment progress.
- **Proof of delivery:** Signed confirmation supports compliance and audits.
- **Invoicing support:** Ensures billing matches actual deliveries.

## Delivery Note Example

While formats vary, a good delivery note always includes:

- Sender and receiver names and addresses
- Order, shipment, and expected delivery dates
- Order reference number
- Quantity of goods delivered

## FAQs

### What is the difference between a delivery note and a packing slip?
A delivery note confirms that goods have been delivered to a customer, including delivery date and receiver details. A packing slip lists items inside a package and focuses on shipment contents rather than delivery confirmation.

### Can a delivery note be a legal document?
A delivery note can act as a legal document if it meets applicable legal requirements and is accepted as proof of delivery in a given transaction.
